Moving to Poland and don't have a PESEL number yet? You can still open a bank
account. Here's exactly how to do it, step by step.

## Do you really need a PESEL?

Many traditional Polish banks ask for a PESEL, but it isn't always mandatory.
Several banks and fintechs accept a passport or national ID instead, which is
ideal if you've just arrived.

### Documents you'll typically need

- A valid passport or EU national ID
- Proof of address (sometimes optional)
- A phone number for verification

## Best options without a PESEL

Digital banks are usually the fastest route. **Revolut** lets you open an account
in minutes with just a passport, hold EUR alongside PLN, and spend abroad at
great rates — perfect for newcomers.

## Step-by-step

1. Download the bank's app or open its website.
2. Enter your details and upload your ID.
3. Complete identity verification (often via a short video).
4. Start using your virtual card immediately.

## After you get your PESEL

Once you receive your PESEL, you can upgrade to a traditional account if you want
features like a mortgage or local credit. Until then, a foreigner-friendly
account covers everyday banking comfortably.
