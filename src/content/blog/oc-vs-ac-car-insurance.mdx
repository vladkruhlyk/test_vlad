If you own a car in Poland, you'll come across two abbreviations constantly: OC
and AC. Understanding the difference can save you money and a lot of stress.

## What is OC insurance?

OC (odpowiedzialność cywilna) is mandatory third-party liability insurance. It
covers damage you cause to other people and their property. Every registered
vehicle must have it — driving without OC carries heavy fines.

### What OC does not cover

OC protects others, not you. If you damage your own car in an at-fault accident,
OC won't pay for your repairs.

## What is AC insurance?

AC (autocasco) is optional comprehensive cover. It pays for damage to your own
vehicle from accidents, theft, vandalism and weather — regardless of fault.

## OC vs AC: which do you need?

- **New or financed cars** — AC is strongly recommended (and often required by
  the lender).
- **Older cars** — many owners stick to OC once the car's value is low.
