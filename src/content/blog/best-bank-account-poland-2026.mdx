Choosing a bank account in Poland in 2026 means weighing monthly fees, card
costs, the quality of the mobile app, and the perks that actually matter to you.
We tested the leading personal accounts so you don't have to.

## What makes a great account in 2026

The best accounts share a few traits: a genuinely free monthly plan (or one
that's easy to keep free), zero card fees with light activity, instant payments
via BLIK, and support for Apple Pay and Google Pay. Increasingly, a polished
mobile app is the deciding factor.

### Fees are only part of the story

A "free" account that nickel-and-dimes you on ATM withdrawals or currency
conversion can cost more than a transparent account with a small fee. Always
look at the full fee table, not just the headline number.

We rated each account on fees, usability, support and benefits:

- **mBank eKonto** — the all-rounder, free for active users with an excellent app.
- **ING Konto Direct** — simple, transparent and genuinely free.
- **Santander Konto Jakie Chcę** — best if you want cashback.

## How to switch accounts

Switching is easier than most people expect. Open the new account online, move
your standing orders and direct debits, then close the old one once everything
has cleared. Most banks can do this in under 30 minutes.

## The bottom line

For most people in Poland, a free digital-first account with BLIK and mobile
payments covers everything they need. Use the comparison tool to filter by the
features you care about and apply in minutes.
